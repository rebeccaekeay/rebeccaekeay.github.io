Behavioural Neuroscience
Measure of Sleep What it measures Why we use it
EEG Brain Activity Identifies sleep disorders and problems such as motor seizure disorders
EMG Muscle Activity Identifies muscle disorders, and restless movement during sleep
EOG Eye movement Useful in monitoring REM (restless eye movement) sleep, to monitor level of activity
Narcolepsy

Narcolepsy is a sleep disorder in which a person has an abnormality within their brain which causes them to fall directly into REM sleep.

Hypersomnia

Hypersomnia is a disorder entailing excessive sleepiness.

Sleep Apnea

Sleep Apnea is when someone stops breathing during sleep, this leads to repeated awakenings.

Cataplexy

Catapleaxy is a loss of muscle tone whilst awake causing people to fall over, or be unable to stand upright
